How does it work?

1

Connect your data

Link your GPS watch (Garmin, Suunto, Coros, Polar…) or import your activities. Rockso centralises all your sessions, across all sports.

2

Rockso analyses your load

AI combines your effort data, perceived exertion (RPE), sleep and daily context to continuously assess your fitness state.

3

Apply the recommendations

Receive simple adjustments: stabilise, increase or reduce load, switch to cross-training, or adapt your week to real-life constraints.

What Rockso helps you do

Continuous load analysis

Rockso analyses how your sessions chain together (intensity, volume, frequency) alongside day-to-day stress, and highlights sensitive periods where adjusting training becomes critical to reaching your goals.

Seamless multi-sport

Running, cycling, triathlon, football, padel, tennis, skiing… Rockso brings endurance sports, team sports, racket sports and winter sports into a single view, even when traditional platforms keep them separate.

Actionable recommendations

Instead of leaving you with abstract metrics, Rockso turns your data into simple decisions: increase, stabilise or decrease load, switch to cross-training, adapt a key week to your real-life context.

An approach rooted in sports science

Rockso is built on research in exercise physiology, injury prevention and data science. The goal is not to "predict" the future, but to offer a robust and actionable reading of training load, stress and fatigue over time.

  • Models informed by longitudinal athlete data.
  • Support for multi-sport practice (running, cycling, team sports, etc.).
  • Explainability of the signals behind alerts and recommendations.

Co-building with the field

We are looking for pilot teams, coaches and organisations to confront theory with the reality of everyday training and adapt the models to your constraints.

Your data stays your data

Rockso is designed with a strong focus on confidentiality and data governance. Athletes and organisations remain in control of their data and decide what they want to share and with whom.

  • Secure hosting and separation of environments.
  • Transparency on which data is used by the models.
  • In the future, potential decentralised scenarios (data monetisation, sharing with research projects, etc.) within a controlled framework.

For more details about how we handle personal data, please refer to our privacy policy.
