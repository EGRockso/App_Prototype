Privacy Policy for Rockso

Last Updated: December 3, 2025

1. Introduction

Welcome to Rockso ("we", "our", or "us"). We are committed to protecting your privacy and handling your personal data in an open and transparent manner.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and share your information when you use:

  • the Rockso mobile application,
  • the Rockso web interfaces, including the rockso.app website,
  • and any other related services (collectively, the "Services").

Rockso is an AI-powered multisport training platform focused on injury prevention and sustainable athletic progression. Our Services help amateur athletes optimize their training through intelligent data analysis.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Account Information: Email address, name, date of birth, gender, height, weight.
  • Athletic Profile: Sports practiced, fitness goals, training preferences.
  • Training Data: Manually entered activities, training plans, notes, and perceived exertion ratings (RPE).
  • Communications: Messages, feedback, and support requests.

2.2 Information We Collect Automatically

  • Activity Data from Connected Services: When you connect third-party services (Garmin, Strava, Oura, Whoop, etc.), we may collect:
    • Activity details (sport type, date, duration, distance, elevation).
    • Physiological data (heart rate, heart rate variability, power output, cadence).
    • Recovery data (sleep duration and quality, stress levels).
    • GPS data (location tracks during activities).
  • Device Information: Device type, operating system, unique device identifiers, mobile network information.
  • Usage Data: How you interact with our Services, features used, time spent, error logs.

2.3 Information from Third-Party Services

When you authorize connections to third-party platforms, we may access:

  • Garmin Connect: Activities, heart rate, HRV, sleep, stress.
  • Strava: Activity summaries (for export purposes only).
  • Oura Ring: Sleep, HRV, recovery scores.
  • Whoop: Strain, recovery, sleep.
  • Other connected services: As specified during authorization.

Important: We only access data you explicitly authorize through OAuth 2.0. You can revoke access at any time through our app settings or directly with the third-party provider.

3. How We Use Your Information

3.1 Primary Purposes

  • Training Analysis: Calculate training load, assess injury risk, analyze performance trends.
  • Personalized Recommendations: Provide AI-powered training adjustments and recovery guidance.
  • Injury Prevention: Identify overtraining patterns and suggest preventive measures.
  • Service Functionality: Sync data across devices, maintain your training plans, enable notifications.

3.2 Secondary Purposes

  • Product Improvement: Analyze usage patterns to enhance features and user experience.
  • Customer Support: Respond to inquiries, troubleshoot issues, provide assistance.
  • Security: Detect and prevent fraud, abuse, and security incidents.
  • Legal Compliance: Comply with applicable laws, regulations, and legal processes.

3.3 Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning

We use AI and machine learning technologies to:

  • Predict injury risk based on training load patterns.
  • Generate personalized training recommendations.
  • Extract insights from unstructured data (e.g., training notes, RPE feedback).
  • Optimize training plans based on your physiological responses.

Your data may be used to improve our AI models, but only in aggregated, anonymized form unless you provide explicit consent for research purposes.

4. How We Share Your Information

4.1 With Your Consent

  • Strava Export: When you choose to export activities created in Rockso to your Strava account.
  • Coach Access: If you connect with a coach through our platform, they can access your training data as authorized by you.
  • Research Partners: Only with your explicit opt-in consent, we may share anonymized, aggregated data with sports science research institutions.

4.2 Service Providers

We share data with trusted third-party service providers who assist us in:

  • Cloud Hosting: For example, Supabase (EU-based servers for GDPR compliance, if applicable).
  • Authentication: Supabase Auth or equivalent for secure login and OAuth management.
  • AI Services: Anthropic (Claude), OpenAI (for premium features), or similar providers.
  • Analytics: Usage analytics providers (anonymized data only).
  • Payment Processing: Stripe or similar services (for subscription billing).

All service providers are contractually bound to protect your data and use it only for specified purposes.

4.3 Legal Requirements

We may disclose your information if required by law, court order, or government regulation, or if we believe disclosure is necessary to:

  • Comply with legal obligations.
  • Protect our rights, property, or safety.
  • Prevent fraud or security threats.
  • Enforce our Terms of Service.

4.4 Business Transfers

In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred to the acquiring entity. We will notify you before your data becomes subject to a different privacy policy.

5. Data We Do Not Share or Sell

We NEVER:

  • Sell your personal data to third parties.
  • Share raw Garmin, Oura, or Whoop data with external platforms (except as you explicitly authorize, e.g., Strava export).
  • Provide individual user data to advertisers.
  • Monetize your physiological data.

6. Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your information:

  • Encryption: All data in transit uses HTTPS/TLS encryption; sensitive data at rest (e.g., OAuth tokens) is encrypted using strong encryption (e.g., AES-256).
  • Access Controls: Role-based access with the principle of least privilege.
  • Authentication: Secure password hashing (e.g., bcrypt), JWT tokens with short expiry.
  • Regular Security Audits: Ongoing monitoring for vulnerabilities.
  • EU-Based Hosting: Where applicable, data stored on GDPR-compliant servers in the European Union.

However, no system is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security, but we are committed to protecting your data to the best of our ability.

7. Data Retention

  • Active Accounts: We retain your data as long as your account is active and for as long as necessary to provide the Services.
  • Inactive Accounts: If you do not use Rockso for 24 months, we may delete non-essential data after notifying you.
  • After Account Deletion: Most data is permanently deleted within 30 days; some data may be retained longer for legal compliance (e.g., billing records for tax purposes).
  • Anonymized Data: Aggregated, anonymized data may be retained indefinitely for research and product improvement.

8. Your Rights (GDPR & CCPA)

You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

8.1 Access & Portability

  • Right to Access: Request a copy of all personal data we hold about you.
  • Data Portability: Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format (e.g., JSON or CSV).

8.2 Correction & Deletion

  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"): Request deletion of your personal data.

8.3 Control & Restriction

  • Right to Restrict Processing: Limit how we use your data.
  • Right to Object: Object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Revoke consent for data processing at any time (e.g., disconnect Garmin).

8.4 How to Exercise Your Rights

You can exercise your rights via:

  • In-App: Settings > Privacy & Data > Manage My Data (when available).
  • Email: privacy@rockso.app (we will respond within 30 days, where required by law).
  • Account Deletion: Settings > Account > Delete My Account (this action is irreversible).

For EU residents: You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your national data protection authority.

9. Children's Privacy

Rockso is not intended for users under 18 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we discover we have collected data from a child, we will delete it promptly.

If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us immediately.

10. International Data Transfers

Rockso is operated from the European Union. If you access our Services from outside the EU:

  • Your data may be transferred to and processed in the EU.
  • We use appropriate safeguards (such as standard contractual clauses) where required by law.
  • By using Rockso, you consent to this transfer.

11. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use minimal tracking technologies:

  • Essential Cookies: Required for authentication and session management.
  • Analytics: Anonymized usage statistics to improve our Services (you may be able to opt out in Settings or via your browser).
  • No Advertising Cookies: We do not use cookies for targeted advertising.

12. Third-Party Links and Services

Our Services may contain links to third-party websites or integrate with third-party services (e.g., Garmin, Strava). We are not responsible for their privacy practices. We encourage you to review their privacy policies before providing any information.

13. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we will:

  • Notify you via email or in-app notification, where appropriate.
  • Update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy.
  • Provide a prominent notice in the app or on the website.

Continued use of Rockso after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

15. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR)

We process your personal data under the following legal bases:

  • Contract Performance: To provide Services you have requested (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR).
  • Consent: For optional features like research data sharing (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR).
  • Legitimate Interests: To improve our Services, ensure security, and prevent fraud (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR).
  • Legal Obligation: To comply with laws and regulations (Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR).

By using Rockso, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to this Privacy Policy.
